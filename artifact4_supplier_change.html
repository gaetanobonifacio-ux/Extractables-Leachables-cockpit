The change reduces the known NDBA pathway but introduces a new, uncharacterized nitrosamine pathway and a hotter cure.

Removing the dibutylamine accelerator is expected to lower NDBA at source. However, the replacement ZBEC accelerator carries a dibenzylamine moiety, a precursor for N-nitroso-dibenzylamine (NDBzA), which is not in the current 11-compound analytical panel, and the cure temperature rises from 165 to 170 C, a recognized nitrosamine-formation factor. Net nitrosamine risk cannot be concluded as reduced until the new grade is characterized under sponsor conditions.
